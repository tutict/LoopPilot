# Illustrative Research Trace

> This is a fictional trace for explaining LoopPilot behavior. It does not represent
> real browsing, real sources, or verified claims.

## User Request

> Determine the effective date of a hypothetical municipal packaging rule and write
> a short answer with reliable sources. Continue until conflicts are resolved or the
> remaining uncertainty is explicit.

## Goal

**Objective:** Produce a concise, source-backed statement about the rule's effective
date without hiding material uncertainty.

**Deliverables:** A short answer, source list, conflict analysis, and verification
notes.

**Constraints:** Prefer primary sources; distinguish enactment, effective, and
enforcement dates; do not present illustrative sources as real.

**Success criteria:**

1. The answer defines which date it reports.
2. At least one authoritative primary source supports the date.
3. Material source conflicts are investigated, not averaged away.
4. Publication and update dates are considered.
5. Any unresolved uncertainty is visible in the final answer.

**Available evidence:** In this fictional scenario, assume access to an official rule
text, an agency FAQ, and a secondary news summary.

**Unresolved blockers:** None at intake.

## Initial Native Plan

1. Locate the enacted rule and identify its date language.
2. Check current agency implementation guidance.
3. Use a reputable secondary source to cross-check interpretation.
4. Reconcile terminology and dates.
5. Draft and verify every factual claim against the collected evidence.

## Execution and First Verification

**Illustrative observation:** The fictional official rule says it becomes effective
on January 1, while the fictional news summary says businesses have until July 1.

**Illustrative verification:** The dates conflict if both are described as the
"start date." The first research pass therefore fails success criterion 3. Reporting
either date without clarification would overstate the evidence.

## Replanning After the Source Conflict

Classify the conflict as new evidence, not a tool failure. Revise the existing plan:

1. Inspect definitions and transitional clauses in the rule text.
2. Check whether the agency FAQ distinguishes legal effect from enforcement.
3. Compare document publication and revision dates.
4. Draft separate claims for effective and enforcement dates if supported.

## Second Execution and Verification

**Illustrative observation:** The fictional transition clause preserves January 1
as the legal effective date, while the newer fictional agency FAQ describes an
enforcement grace period ending June 30. The news summary collapsed those concepts.

**Illustrative evidence check:**

- Primary rule text supports the legal effective date.
- Current agency guidance supports the enforcement timing.
- The secondary account is retained only as context and is not used to override the
  primary sources.
- The answer names both concepts and their source dates.

## Ending Report

```text
Status: Completed (illustrative scenario only)
Completed work: concise answer distinguishing legal effect from enforcement timing.
Verification evidence: illustrative comparison of rule text, transition clause, agency FAQ,
and publication dates.
Remaining gaps: none within the closed fictional scenario; no real-world research
was performed.
Stop reason: the scenario's conflict is resolved at the terminology level.
Best next action: use actual primary documents before making a real-world claim.
```
